# Office Audio Capture and Knowledge Pipeline Design  

The week’s work defined a unified architecture that treats raw office audio as an immutable asset while enabling reproducible AI processing. The investigation focused on device selection, data‑model hierarchy, model‑version traceability, and a governed knowledge‑candidate workflow.

## Audio Capture Infrastructure: Device Matrix and Pipeline  

The design specifies a five‑stage pipeline—Capture → Raw Audio Storage → Metadata → Speech‑to‑Text (STT) → Conversation → Knowledge Candidate → Knowledge Graph → Manual/FAQ/SOP. A comparison of 100–200 device models resulted in three deployment tiers: (1) Raspberry Pi 5 + ReSpeaker USB mic (initial cost ¥20‑50 k, 24 h operation, Linux/USB, low power), (2) meeting‑room units such as Meeting Owl 3, Jabra Speak2 75, Poly Sync 60, Yamaha YVC (initial cost ¥50‑250 k), and (3) ceiling‑mount solutions like Shure MXA920, Sennheiser TeamConnect Ceiling, Yamaha RM‑CG (initial cost ¥300 k‑1.5 M). All devices must expose an API/SDK, support PoE or LAN, and be AI‑compatible. The PoC targets 1‑5 participants using the Pi tier, with a roadmap that scales to full‑office ceiling deployment by Phase 4.

## Data Model and Storage Hierarchy  

Both documents converge on a flat hierarchical layout: `raw_audio`, `processed_audio`, `transcripts`, `conversation_units`, `knowledge_candidates`, `approved_knowledge`. Filenames follow the convention `YYYYMMDD‑HHMMSS‑HHMMSS‑room‑device.wav`. Required metadata includes recording_id, room, device, timestamps, checksum, duration, sample_rate, firmware, microphone_model, and for STT results: model, version, prompt, confidence, speaker_id. The immutable raw audio storage satisfies the principle that “生音声は捨てない” (raw audio is never discarded), while all downstream artifacts are version‑controlled to enable re‑processing.

## Speech‑to‑Text Benchmark and Model Versioning  

A dedicated STT benchmark will compare Whisper‑based models against proprietary alternatives, recording confidence scores and processing latency. Each transcript stores the model name, version, and prompt identifier, allowing future model updates to re‑run the pipeline without loss of provenance. The pipeline enforces that “AI処理結果はいつでも再生成できる” (AI results can be regenerated) by retaining the original WAV files and all version metadata.

## Knowledge Candidate Extraction and Review Workflow  

The knowledge‑candidate step tags conversation units with types such as workaround, decision rule, exception handling, or hidden caveat, and outputs JSON cards containing title, content, evidence, confidence, and a link to the exact audio segment (recording_id, start/end offsets). Markdown cards are generated with status “未レビュー”. An AI integration layer clusters similar cards, flags duplicates, and proposes manual updates. Human reviewers operate on AI‑ranked candidates only, using statuses approved / needs_edit / duplicate / wrong / sensitive / irrelevant / pending. Success metrics include < 5 % recording loss, > 90 % transcript completion, 3‑20 candidates per day, < 30 min weekly review time, and 1‑5 approved cards per week.

## Security, Privacy, and Legal Controls  

The security design mandates explicit recording notices, defined capture zones, and access‑log auditing. Personal and customer identifiers are masked by default, and sensitive conversation segments are isolated from the knowledge‑extraction path. Legal and privacy specifications require retention of raw audio for quality assurance while limiting downstream distribution to approved knowledge artifacts. Network design includes VLAN segmentation for capture devices and Kubernetes‑based operation to enforce least‑privilege access.

**Synthesis** – The combined architecture demonstrates that a disciplined separation of capture, immutable storage, and reproducible AI processing creates a durable knowledge asset. By embedding model‑version traceability and a controlled review loop, the system supports incremental improvement of AI capabilities without re‑collecting data, positioning the organization to convert routine office dialogue into strategic, auditable knowledge.
